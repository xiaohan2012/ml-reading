\section{Conclusion}

We present \name, a modular and efficient framework for temporal graph learning built around a novel hook formalism. By decoupling graph operations from model logic, \name enables rapid prototyping and code reuse, unifying CTDG and DTDG methods under a single research-ready library. Efficiency-wise, \name is highly competitive and on average $7.8\times$ faster in training than the widely used DyGLib. 
We ultimately envision \name as a foundation for a shared ecosystem where models, hooks, and analytics can be seamlessly composed and reused, accelerating TGL research.
